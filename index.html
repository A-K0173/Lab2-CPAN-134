The Best vs The Worst Shark Tank Pitch

The Best Pitch: TheMagic5
The Worst Pitch: PDX Pet Design

The Best Shark Tank Pitch

One of the best Shark Tank pitches is for a company called TheMagic5.
They create custom fitted swimming googles, tailored specifically for the person who ordered them.
Here is a list of reasons why this was such a good pitch in Shark Tank.

  1. The founders, Rasmus Barfred and Bo Haaber, had a clear vision for their company.
    This allowed them to clearly show the sharks they had a solution to a real problem, and they had the marketing down.
  2. The founders were also passionate about their product.
    This made the sharks more interested in potentially investing into their company.
  3. The Sharks saw potential in the product to be a huge success.
    This is why the Sharks started to counter eachother's offers becausr they all wanted to invest into the company.

The Worst Shark Tank Pitch

One of the worst Shark Tank pitches is unfortunately for a company called PDX Pet Design.
The founders, Tara and Jason, asked for $300,000 for 15% of their company, and only had two main products.
Here is a list of reasons why this pitch didn't work out for them.

  1. The sharks were not impressed by the pitch. Lori, Mark and Robert all passed on the deal.
    Mr. Wonderful actually offered to invest, but it was $50,000 for %20, which was turned down by the founders.
  2. The founders asked for too much money.
    They asked $300,000 for 15% of their company which the sharks found was too high.
  3. The founders didn't have a strong business plan. They didn't have a clear plan and their products were unfortunately not unique enough and were lacking.
    In addition to this, the sharks were pretty disgusted by the "Licky Brush" anyway, which further led to them not being able to survive in the Shark Tank.
